link to [productivity is systemic]()

"being productive" is about flow, from concept to cash (from Poppendieck)

who to involve?

agile/scrum/SAFe tends to create stricter team boundaries, e.g. work is mediated by a product owner. This risks more local optimization. 
Doing VSM with product management/business involved helps everyone see the whole. Enables developers to better understand what is valuable.

# Value Stream Mapping in practice

Some examples of what we have seen
- productizing research in a research institute
- flow stalling at pull request reviews 
- granularity of work (3)

Cooperative teams tend to focus more on local optimization; VSM helps to see the whole, stimulate more collaboration.

Being busy / utilized is not the goal (although you might feel that you get paid for doing stuff). Does not mean just sitting there (although just sitting there and observing what's happening can be very useful!) but stepping in to help a colleague if that improves overall flow. If you just continue working on your part, you will just increase work in progress

VSM is a way of zooming out; helps participants to look at the whole, not just their part

VSM is a helps starting conversations about: optimize delivery of value; prevent local optimization; work more collaboratively
